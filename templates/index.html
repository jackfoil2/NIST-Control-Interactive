NIST CONTROL FAMILIES

The NIST (National Institute of Standards and Technology) Special Publication 800-53 defines security and privacy controls used to protect information systems. These controls are categorized into different families, each addressing a key security area.

Access Control

Defines who can access systems and what actions they are authorized to perform, ensuring proper restrictions are in place.

Awarness and Training

Focuses on educating personnel to recognize and respond to cybersecurity threats effectively.

Audit and Accountability

Ensures systems record user activities to enable accountability and detect security violations.

Assessment, Authorization, and Monitoring

Covers security assessments, system authorizations, and continuous monitoring of systems.

Configuration Management

Establishes guidelines for controlling system configurations to ensure consistency and security.

Contingency Planning

Ensures systems and organizations are prepared to recover from disruptions, disasters, or attacks.
